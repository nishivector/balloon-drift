# Brief: balloon-drift

**Round:** 18
**Protagonist:** Dot — a red heart-shaped helium balloon that slipped from a little girl's hand at her 7th birthday party. She's been rising ever since.
**Mechanic verb:** STEER
**One line:** Tap left or right to nudge Dot through a suburban sky full of power lines, branches, and pigeons — the higher she goes, the more beautiful and dangerous it gets.

**Backstory:** Maya was holding Dot when the wind came. One moment there was a small hand, then there wasn't. Dot doesn't know what she's flying toward. She just keeps going up. This is the whole game.

**World:** A sunny afternoon suburban sky — seen from Dot's perspective rising. Start at rooftop level (chimneys, TV antennas, laundry lines). Rise through telephone poles and trees. Then open blue sky with power lines getting sparse. Then clouds. Then something above the clouds that feels like an answer.

**Mechanic:**
- Dot rises automatically at increasing speed
- Tap left / tap right to steer horizontally (gentle nudge, slight inertia)
- Obstacles: power lines (horizontal, deadly), tree branches (sticky — slow and kill), pigeons (scatter on approach, one direct hit = pop), airplane contrails at high altitude
- The world gets more open and beautiful as you rise — early levels crowded, later levels sparse and peaceful
- Pop = instant death. No HP. One chance.

**Background:** Warm afternoon sky blue `#87AECF` at start, shifting to deeper `#4A7FC4` at altitude
**BPM:** 72, wistful and gentle — gets more layered and orchestral as altitude increases

**Music direction:** Starts as a simple music box melody — like a lullaby, slightly sad, unmistakably a birthday. As Dot rises, instruments gradually layer in: a soft guitar, a cello, eventually strings. The music builds like Dot is becoming something. When she pops, the music box plays one last note and stops. The hook is the melody itself — simple enough to hum, emotional enough to feel.
